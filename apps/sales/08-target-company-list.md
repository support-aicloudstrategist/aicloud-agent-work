---

## How to use this list

1. **For each company**, find the engineering leader on their website (/about, /team) or via Google Search: `site:companyname.com CTO OR "VP Engineering" OR "Head of Infrastructure"`
2. **Guess the email** using the most common format for that company (see patterns below)
3. **Verify the email** — append it to the company's domain, run through [Mail Tester](https://www.mail-tester.com/) or just send; bounces tell you the format is wrong (try the next pattern)
4. **Personalise the first line** — check their Twitter/X, recent blog posts, or funding announcements for a hook
5. **Send Email 1** from the cold-email-sequence doc
6. **Log in Vikunja tracker** — `vk add 7 "Reached out to {firstname} at {company}" "Email pattern: {pattern}. Personalisation: {hook}."`

---

## Email pattern cheat sheet (try in this order)

1. `firstname@company.com` (most common for Indian startups)
2. `firstname.lastname@company.com`
3. `f.lastname@company.com`
4. `flastname@company.com`
5. `firstname_lastname@company.com`

**To verify:** Hunter.io free tier (25/month) gives you the known pattern per domain. Or use email-verifier.io. Or just send and watch for bounce.

## Personalization hooks to look for

For each target, spend 5 minutes researching:

1. **Last funding round date + amount** — "Congrats on the Series B" (only if recent, <3 months)
2. **Recent hiring** — "Saw you're hiring X platform engineers" (infra scaling signal)
3. **Recent engineering blog post** — "Read your post on {topic}" (trust signal)
4. **CEO/CTO Twitter/X activity** — "Your thread on {topic} last week"
5. **Product launch** — "Saw the {product} launch"
6. **Conference talk** — "Caught your talk at {event}"
7. **GitHub activity** — "Saw your team's open-source work on {project}"
8. **Podcast appearance** — "Listened to your episode of {podcast}"

If you can't find ONE specific hook, move on. Generic cold email is worse than no email.
