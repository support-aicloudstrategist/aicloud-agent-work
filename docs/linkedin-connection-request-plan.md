# LinkedIn Connection Request Plan — First 30 Days

**For:** Anushka B, Founder · AICloudStrategist
**Objective:** Build a warm network of 500+ relevant, targeted connections in 30 days without triggering LinkedIn's anti-spam systems
**LinkedIn rate limits:** ~100 connection requests per week before warnings; stay below 15 per day to be safe

---

## Rules (Hard Constraints)

1. **15 connection requests per day maximum.** LinkedIn throttles or bans profiles that exceed this consistently.
2. **Always include a personalised note.** Blank requests have a 3–5% acceptance rate. Personalised notes see 30–50%.
3. **Never reference "lead generation" or "sales" in the note.** LinkedIn's spam filters and recipients both screen for these words.
4. **Never send a paste-identical note to every person.** Vary at minimum the first sentence.
5. **Max 300 characters in the note.** LinkedIn's hard limit.
6. **If a request is not accepted in 14 days, withdraw it.** Reduces pending-request clutter and protects your profile reputation score.

## Daily Execution Checklist

Every weekday during the 30-day push:

1. **Batch-build** — spend 15 minutes the night before identifying that day's 15 targets. Copy their first name, company, and role into a spreadsheet.
2. **Batch-send** — spend 15 minutes the next day sending requests with personalised first sentences.
3. **Accept-back loop** — every morning, check notifications. If anyone accepts, send a thank-you + useful link within 24 hours (see Day-2 message below).
4. **Stay under 15** — LinkedIn tracks daily request velocity; exceeding 15 consistently triggers the "You're Inviting People You Don't Know" warning, which halves acceptance rate.

## Red Flags — When to Pause

Pause sending and let the account cool down for 3–5 days if:

- LinkedIn shows "You're Inviting People You Don't Know" warning
- Acceptance rate drops below 10% over a week (your note text may be triggering spam filters)
- You receive multiple "reported as spam" signals (LinkedIn hides this but you'll see restrictions appear)
- Profile views plummet or search visibility drops

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not use LinkedIn automation tools (Dux-Soup, Phantombuster, Meet Alfred). LinkedIn detects these and permanently restricts profiles.
- Do not send connection requests and immediately message a pitch. Day-2 message is the minimum gap.
- Do not mass-follow-up on requests older than 14 days — withdraw them.
- Do not buy "LinkedIn lead lists" or imported contact dumps. Your account gets flagged and half the leads are stale.
