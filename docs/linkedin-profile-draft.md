## About (2,600 char limit)

> I founded AICloudStrategist to close a gap I kept seeing in the Indian mid-market: companies running ₹5–20L/month cloud bills without senior-architect attention on the cost side.
>
> **Background.** 7+ years in DevOps, cloud engineering, and automation across startups and enterprise environments. Hands-on across AWS, Azure, and GCP — Kubernetes rightsizing, Terraform IaC, observability stacks, CI/CD pipelines, and the FinOps practices that tie cost to engineering decisions.
>
> **The problem I keep seeing:**
>
> Companies that moved to cloud during 2020–2022 under growth pressure. The architecture made sense then. Three years of unreviewed auto-scaling, storage tiers, and provisioning later — 15 to 30 percent of the bill is avoidable. It does not appear as a flagged line item. It compounds quietly.
>
> The Indian mid-market founder or CFO running a ₹5–20L/month bill rarely gets senior-architect attention. They get a vendor call and a 20-page proposal that no one acts on.
>
> **How AICloudStrategist is built differently:**
>
> → **Founder-led. Enterprise-reviewed.** Founder delivery paired with senior-architect oversight — 22+ years of Fortune 500 and global-consulting weight in every review.
> → 4-week fixed scope. 20–30% verified reduction.
> → Open-source tooling (OpenCost, Infracost, OpenTelemetry). No licence lock-in, full audit trail.
> → INR-first pricing. Transparent gain-share on verified savings.
>
> **Typical findings in a first review:**
>
> • Reserved Instance coverage below 30% on stable workloads (should be 70%+)
> • S3 / Blob storage in hot tiers holding data untouched for 12+ months
> • GKE / EKS node pools sized for peak QPS that hits twice a quarter
> • Cross-region egress for workloads that could run in source region
> • Orphaned EBS volumes, unattached disks, and stale snapshots
>
