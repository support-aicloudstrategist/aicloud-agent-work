# Subject Line Variants — Email 1 (Cold Outreach)

**Audience:** Engineering managers, CTOs, DevOps leads at Indian mid-market SaaS/tech companies  
**Goal:** First-touch cold email, AWS FinOps services  
**Constraints:** ≤55 chars · no hype words · no "free" · Indian business tone

---

## Winners

These two are predicted highest open rate based on personalization, specificity, and credibility signals.

| # | Subject Line | Chars | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| W1 | `{{First_Name}}, AWS gap we see in most Indian SaaS` | 51* | First-name personalization + "we see" implies real data, not a pitch; curiosity gap |
| W2 | `3 findings from a recent ap-south-1 cost audit` | 47 | Specific number + specific region + "recent" = credible, timely, low sales-pressure |

*Rendered length after merge: ~42 chars

---

## Control Variants

| # | Subject Line | Chars | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | `AWS cost review — no pitch, just observations` | 45 | Anti-sales framing; disarms skepticism upfront |
| C2 | `Your cloud spend vs Indian mid-market peers` | 43 | Benchmarking angle; peer comparison is high-pull in Indian B2B |
| C3 | `S3 Glacier Instant: one rule, 80% cost drop` | 44 | Specific tactic + verified stat (₹0.025→₹0.005/GB); shows competence |
| C4 | `EBS governance gap — where Indian SaaS teams lag` | 49 | Problem-framing; "lag" signals a known pattern, not a sales hook |
| C5 | `FinOps note for {{Company}}'s engineering team` | 46 | Company-personalised; positions as a note, not an offer |
| C6 | `Quick question about your ap-south-1 account` | 45 | Direct question format; low friction, implies something specific to them |

---

## Testing guidance

- A/B W1 vs W2 first (50/50 split, n ≥ 200 per arm)
- Promote winner against C3 and C4 in round 2 (data + problem frame best complement)
- Avoid C6 if list is cold and unverified — "question about your account" can misfire without prior signal
