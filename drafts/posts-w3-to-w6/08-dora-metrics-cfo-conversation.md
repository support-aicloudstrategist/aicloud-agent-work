# LinkedIn Post — DORA Metrics as a CFO Conversation

CFOs don't care about deployment frequency.

They care about: How fast can we ship revenue? How much does downtime cost? Are we getting value from the ₹2Cr we're spending on engineering tooling?

DORA metrics answer all three — if you translate them correctly.

**The translation table:**

| DORA Metric | What it actually measures | CFO question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| Deployment Frequency | Engineering throughput | Is the pipeline delivering? |
| Lead Time for Changes | Feature-to-revenue lag | How long does ₹1 of roadmap take to reach users? |
| Change Failure Rate | Quality cost | What % of releases create incidents we pay to fix? |
| Mean Time to Recovery | Downtime cost exposure | What's our downtime liability per incident? |

A 1-hour outage for a ₹500Cr GMV e-commerce platform in India = ~₹14L in lost transactions. If your MTTR is 4 hours and you have 3 incidents/month, that's a board-level number.

Elite DORA performers (top 25%): deploy on demand, <1 hour lead time, <5% change failure rate, <1 hour MTTR.

Your team's DORA score is an engineering story. Reframe it as a finance story and it gets budget and attention.
