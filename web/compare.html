How we compare

An honest look at your four realistic options for a cloud cost or architecture engagement — including when we are not the right fit. We would rather tell you that upfront than 12 weeks in.

Option 1 — Big-4 consultancy (Deloitte, KPMG, EY, PwC) or a Tier-1 systems integrator

Typical engagement: 3–6 months minimum. ₹40L–₹1.5Cr. A partner plus 4–8 junior consultants, often including offshore analysts, executing against a 60-page scope document.

When this is the right choice: You are a 1000+ person enterprise with a compliance deadline, a Board-mandated digital transformation, or a multi-geography footprint where the Big-4 brand is required for internal political cover. The deliverable is the full-estate transformation with global accountability, not a focused cost reduction.

When it is the wrong choice: You are a 30–300 person Indian company trying to reduce a ₹5–50L/month cloud bill. The fee alone exceeds the 12-month savings. The partner you spoke with is not the person doing the work. The recommendations will be competent but generic — and the cost of executing them falls back on your own team anyway.

Option 2 — Freelance cloud consultant

Typical engagement: Hourly billing at ₹2,000–₹6,000/hour, or a fixed project fee of ₹1–5L. One person, no review layer, no formal process.

When this is the right choice: A narrow, well-scoped problem you can describe in three sentences. A Terraform module rewrite. An S3 lifecycle policy setup. You have the in-house expertise to validate their work and the bandwidth to project-manage.

When it is the wrong choice: Cloud cost optimisation or architecture work at scale. A single consultant, no matter how skilled, cannot peer-review their own findings. There is no enterprise rigour in the process, no structured methodology, and no accountability beyond the individual engagement. When the consultant moves on, the institutional knowledge leaves with them.

Option 3 — Hiring an in-house FinOps or DevOps engineer

Typical cost: ₹30–80L/year fully loaded for a senior FinOps engineer or platform lead in an Indian metro. 3–6 months to hire. 3–6 more months to onboard and produce first measurable results.

When this is the right choice: Your cloud spend is above ₹1Cr/month and your engineering team is 100+ people. The work is continuous, not a one-time project. You can afford to wait 9+ months for the first wins to land. The hire's career trajectory inside your company is real (you have a team for them to eventually lead).

When it is the wrong choice: Your cloud spend is ₹5–50L/month. The savings opportunity is ₹1–15L/month. You cannot justify a ₹5L/month salary for that math. You also cannot find a genuine FinOps engineer in the Indian market right now — the role is newer than the demand.

Option 4 — AICloudStrategist

Typical engagement: ₹75,000 to ₹3.5L for a focused 2–4 week engagement, fixed-scope, fixed-fee. Founder-led delivery with senior-architect oversight on every review. Optional gain-share on verified savings.

When we are the right choice: You are a 30–300 person Indian SMB or mid-market company with ₹5–50L/month cloud spend. The problem is specific — cost optimisation, an architecture review, a DevOps modernisation, an AI infrastructure governance gap. You want an engineer in the room who has actually done the work, a written summary your team can execute against, and a fixed fee with no hourly creep.

When we are the wrong choice: You have a Board mandate that requires Big-4 branding on the engagement letter. You want a team of 20 consultants embedded for a year. Your monthly cloud spend is under ₹2L (savings ceiling is too low for our fee to make sense — we will tell you honestly and point you elsewhere). You want a tool licence you can hide behind, not an engineer who will show you exactly what is wrong.

A practical rule of thumb

If your monthly cloud bill is in the ₹5L to ₹50L band, if you do not have a dedicated FinOps or platform function yet, and if your pain is specific (cost, architecture, DevOps, or AI infrastructure) rather than a full-estate transformation programme — an AICloudStrategist engagement is usually the highest-leverage first move.

If your bill is above ₹1Cr/month with a 150+ person engineering team, consider a long-term hire first and engage us only for the specific 2–4 week sprints the hire does not have bandwidth for (typically the initial cost baseline, a cloud architecture review, or an AI infrastructure governance setup).

If your bill is below ₹2L/month, none of the four options justify the cost. Run the free 30-minute Cloud Cost Health Check and execute the findings yourself — we will tell you which of our lead-magnet PDFs to read.
