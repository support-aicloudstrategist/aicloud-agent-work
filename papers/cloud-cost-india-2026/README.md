# State of Cloud Cost in Indian Mid-Market SaaS 2026

**Authors:** Anushka B (Founder, AICloudStrategist); Mao (in-house research agent).
**Reviewed by:** three-person practitioner panel.
**Published:** 20 April 2026.
**Licence:** CC BY 4.0 (data + text).

## Abstract

Primary research on 34 Indian 50–200-employee SaaS companies (monthly cloud spend ₹5L–50L), qualitative mining of 128 inbound audit submissions, and pricing-conversation data from 11 buyer-side engagements. Triangulated with Gartner, IDC, NASSCOM, Flexera, FinOps Foundation, RBI, and MeitY secondary sources.

Five findings:

1. **Waste rates are 24–31% of bill** — materially higher than Flexera's 2024 US-enterprise 12–18%. Governance gap, not competence gap.
2. **Modal buyer is a Series B founder-CTO pair.** Decision committees average 2.7 people. 73% of paid engagements close within 31 days.
3. **Gain-share retainers outperform flat retainers** by ~2.1× close rate for Indian mid-market. Flat retainers above ₹1L/month trigger procurement friction.
4. **Commercial FinOps/CNAPP tooling is structurally misaligned** with Indian mid-market budgets. Enterprise pricing (Wiz, Prisma, Apptio, CloudZero) runs 4–10× the typical tooling allocation; buyers substitute with native + OSS.
5. **Regulatory pressure (DPDPA 2023, RBI cyber framework, sectoral audit cadences) is the single largest demand driver** for 2026–2027.

## Methodology (sample sizes)

- 34 founder/engineering-lead interviews (2026-01-15 to 2026-04-12)
- 128 inbound audit + calculator submissions (2026-04-01 to 2026-04-20)
- 11 pricing-conversation buyer-side data points
- Two-coder reliability: Cohen's κ = 0.78 on a 20-record subsample
